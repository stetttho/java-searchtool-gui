 After reading zillions of modern day "chick lit" novels, finally comes a novel for mature audiences. Whereas, a good book, is a good book I was overjoyed to see that Ms. McMillan has stood her ground and not just fall into the mainstream of what is selling now.  She has not forsaken the audience that helped her pioneer the new renaissance of African American Literature. This book will do well, and hopefully the publishing industry will realize that the middle-aged African American reader is still alive and kicking; and most importantly purchasing books.  P. Walker Williams (...)
