 In a time when all it takes is glue and someone else's hair to transform a person, this book is right on target for young girls struggling with positive self esteem. The story tells of a young girl who does not understand why her mother has to comb her hair each night.   Through her mother's soothing pulls and calm voice, this young lady understands that her hair is a gift to be cherished. Exceptionally written and magnificently drawn, this book should be added to any library to teach tolerance and self love.
