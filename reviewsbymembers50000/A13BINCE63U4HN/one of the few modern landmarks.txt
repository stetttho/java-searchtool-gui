 Decades down the line when critics have more sense than it takes to rave over "groundbreaking" tripe like "Moulin Rouge" it may be time to re-evaluate "Cyclo." Tran Anh Hung has created what can be cited as one of the two or three truly visionary film of the 1990s (the case can also be made for Kiarostami's "Close Up" and Koker Trilogy, and Bae Yong-Kyun's "The People in White").
