 Once Charles Chaplin reached the mature as artist and having made the whole circle in his fascinating journey about the childhood's dreams, and having made the adorable Lights in the city, he decided to pay hard against the Status Quo in the thirties: Modern Times, Great Dictator, Monsieur Verdoux  are his  bitterest films.  But the wings of the golden ages came to his mind and soul, and Chaplin decides a self portrait. If you are not moved by Calvero's death you are not of this world. A love story that will endure for years and years, no matter what the generation you are talking about. This is the essential feature of a masterpiece.
