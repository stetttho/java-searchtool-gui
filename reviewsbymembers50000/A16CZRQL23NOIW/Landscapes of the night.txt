 The paradigm has always existed. The human brain works out as a tremendously advanced computer. But there is still more.   Napoleon stated once: "The death is a sleep without dreams". In this transitional state we switch off the whole array of defense mechanisms acquired through the eons of evolution. In fact what it happens during sleep serves some vital purpose.   Using the recent findings which attempt to trap the stuff of what dreams may become, the book literally demolishes popular misconceptions and fixed ancestral beliefs , replacing them with this well known premise: our brain considered as a primordial computer.  Once you start to read it, it will hard to leave it until you finish it. A fundamental reference text of perennial consult as well for students, public in general and teachers.
