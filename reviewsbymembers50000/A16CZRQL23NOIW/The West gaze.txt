 After the colossal feat of Rashomon in 1950, and its world repercussion around the world, it would said that this film (the glorious masterpiece of Kurosawa to my mind) modified and influenced the new generations to come of filmmakers about this delicate subject. How many victims have been murdered or condemned due the religious, political or moral truth of the historical Age? But worst still: How many more will be dead due the same reason?    Martin Ritt dared and won with this discrete but potent portrait about the perpetual elusiveness of the truth and its always changing nature according the beholder, not only under the actual circumstances, but through the years, because most of us tend to make our minds and alter our own perspective decade after decade. So when you talk about the truth, it would be convenient to make clear and underline: my truth instead of the truth.
