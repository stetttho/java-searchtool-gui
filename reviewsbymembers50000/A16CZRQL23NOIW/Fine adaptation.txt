 Among the adapted versions translated to screen this is perhaps, the most successful. This classic story deals about an impoverished student living In a rooming house who decides to murder an old pawnbroker. No matter how many times you have watched or read this brutal story. This novel was the true pivot, the great gate to the existentialist movement. Think for instance in Dostoievsky's prodigal son; Albert Camus and the Happy death: you will find similar traces. This Russian adaptation maintains the mystery and sinister poetry, where all the centuries of Czarists oppression would seem emerge with all the potency of a volcanic eruption.  Filmed in glorious black and white to accent the climax.
