 When Charles Darwin sailed from Plymouth in his twenty ones, under Fitz Roy `s command  as naturalist in the Beagle swift-sailing on December 1831 to turn around the world, with the mission of make a set of measures and checks of scientist character, he was turning without knowing, the course of the story. Through these long five years his observations in Las Galapagos and Patagonia suggested him the first hypothesis about the species evolution through the natural selection process. This journey was more than enough to open the sluices of his overflowed imagination and intrepid talent that would lead to him to write "The species origin", a monumental slap in the face to the ancient beliefs. Hemleben rebuilds step by step the civil and intellectual biography of this singular innovator and undeniable herald of the contemporary age.
