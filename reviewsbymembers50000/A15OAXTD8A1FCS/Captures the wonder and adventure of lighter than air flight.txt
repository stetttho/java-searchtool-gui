 Professor William Waterman Sherman is a burned out teacher who wants to get away from it all. So he builds a giant balloon that can hold him and a years supply of food. Then one day he sets off from San Francisco across the Pacific Ocean. 5 weeks later a ship finds him out in the mid-Atlantic surrounded by wreckage & 20 large balloons. How did he get there? Where did he get the other balloons? The answers to these questions are the subject of this book.  Written in 1948, the story takes place at the end of the 19th century when balloonists were on the cutting edge of science, this book captures the wonder and adventure of lighter than air flight at that time. It is illustrated with delightful drawings by the author and won the Newberry Award.
