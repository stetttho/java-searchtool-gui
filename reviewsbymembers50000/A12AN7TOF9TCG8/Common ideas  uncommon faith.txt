 What if you could get a quick reference to quotes from the world's religions on an array of issues, from murder to Creation?And what if the similarities were striking? Christianity, Buddhism, Islam, Judaism, Sikhism, even Native American religions are represented in this volume. The reader can't help but be hopeful and inspired by the inherent message: On many things, we're not so far apart. Moses expanded this book in 2002, after the evnts of Sept. 11, and the event looms large as one reads it. Perhaps "Oneness" won't settle the debate over whether Islam is, at its core, a religion of tolerance or hate, but it offers helpful glimpses of a faith now at the heart of America's consciousness.
