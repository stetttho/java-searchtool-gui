 Stories full of gritty, realistic characters. Plots that hurtle forward at breakneck speed. Unpredictable twists, even for silverback mystery readers ... John Lescroart is quite simply one of the most underappreciated best-sellers in American letters today. His Dismas Hardy/Abe Glitsky series is as sharp as Turow and Grisham, and "The Motive" does nothing to dim the light that rightfully shines on his work. There should be more. If you haven't yet picked up a Lescroart crime fiction, "The Motive" isn't a bad place to start.
