 Avenging the loss of his Battalion's colours, Richard Sharpe and his trusted friend Sergeant Patrick Harper, set out to capture a French battle standard... an Eagle. In Sharpe's Eagle, Cornwell again gives us the detail, and the fearsome exhilaration and confusion of personal combat and brings it to life.  As with Sharpe's Rifles, I couldn't put this one down.  As soon as I read it, I had to go to the library and get the next one.
