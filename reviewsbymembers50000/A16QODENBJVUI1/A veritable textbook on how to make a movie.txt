 In 1935 the British film industry lagged far behind Hollywood in technical expertise, and that gap somewhat mars this film.  But Hitchcock far exceeded anyone working in Hollywood at the time in his knowledge and  mastery of his craft, so that while the film might be a bit grainier and  the sound a bit muddier than contemporary American releases, THE 39 STEPS  reflects a mastery of craft and film technique largely neglected in the  U.S.
