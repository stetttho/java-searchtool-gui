 Joseph Stalin had a paranoid personality. Nikita Krushchev, one of Stalin's successors as Soviet dictator, could look back on terrifying times with Stalin. He said that Stalin was "sickly suspicious." Stalin imputed his own cruel motives to the people around him. This made him terrified of other people and drove him to seek unassailable power over everyone. And after he came to supreme power he never tired of killing real people for imaginary threats. Read about him in this riveting story of his career as the most prolific murderer in all of human history.
