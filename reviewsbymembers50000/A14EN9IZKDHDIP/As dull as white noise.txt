 The central character, Jack Gladney, is an academic and an authority on Hitler who has trouble speaking German. He and his wife, Babette, have several children, residue of their past marriages to others. All of the children, however young, talk the same as all of the adults, however old. There is, in other words, no individuality in the dialogue. A toxic cloud enveloped part of the imaginary village where Jack and Babette live, but I didn't care, because there are no engaging characters in the book....END
