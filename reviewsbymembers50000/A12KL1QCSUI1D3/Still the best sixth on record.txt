 Even after all these years this still remains the most powerful recording of Mahlers sixth. The seventh from the same cycle is also equally fine and also still outclasses subsequent recordings. In the case of the sixth, however, Bernstein is run quite close by the outstanding Szell Cleveland version, both of which are vastly preferable to the shallow and insipid recordings by Karajan, who was temperamentally unsuited to Mahler's music.
