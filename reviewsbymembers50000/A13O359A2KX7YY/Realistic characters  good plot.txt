 This Boston based novel is a straightforward whodunit that is well written, cleverly plotted and easy to read. The narration is provided by attorney Brady Coyne.  An important client of his, Judge Chester Popowski has been threatened with blackmail.  When the blackmailer is murdered, Coyne finds himself high up on the police's list of suspects. Author William Tapply is obviously a keen observer of human behavior.  The characters in Client Privilege are depicted in a realistic fashion, complete with flaws and frailities. Moreover, the plentiful dialogue rings quite true. Client Privilege is an enjoyable murder mystery written with lots of insight into the human condition. A solid 4 stars.
