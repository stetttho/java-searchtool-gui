 This first person account of an adult survivor of a parent with Munchausen bt proxy is riveting like a train wreck. Some parts are so hard to read, but it is impossible to stop. Julie Gregory gives and unflinching and sometimes unemotional account of the abuse she suffered at the hand of her mother. The final pages are more representative of how she really seems to feel. An interesting novel about a very rare and often missed mental illness of a parent which manifests itself on their child.
