 If history texts were written more like Shoals of Time, we'd all probably find ourselves more engrossed in the fascinating subjects of our past. This book reads almost like a novel and it drew me in right at the first chapter. Gavan Daws weaves a colorful tale from his well-researched sources cited in his notes.  Alas, it does not delve into Hawaiian history before the arrival of Cooke. What a tragedy for us all; Gavan Daws might have held us spellbound even longer.
