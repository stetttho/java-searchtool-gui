 Gardner comes across as a realist and an inspiration to writers.  This is not a step-by-step book about how to write a novel.  It is more of a big picture approach to developing the mindset, skills and discipline to become a novelist.   Gardner illustrates the ingredients to writing novels and to being a novelist.  His examples of his own writing experiences are the crux of this book.  He has been through the angst and exhiliration of writing and shares his insights in this book. He clearly conveys that writing takes discipline, skill, perseverance and teachability.  He elaborates on these areas in the book. Overall, the book is inspiring and educational.  It is not drawn out or wordy.  He gets to the point and keeps it short, so it is very helpful throughout.
