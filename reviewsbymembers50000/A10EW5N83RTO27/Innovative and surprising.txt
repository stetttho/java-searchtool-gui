 Perhaps only someone from the ex-Soviet Union could have written a book in which the Jewish experience in the 20th Century receives an innovative analysis.  It takes courage as well as fidelity to the truth to tackle the question of Jewish enthusiasm for Bolshevism and even Stalinism in the USSR and elsewhere until the evils of Stalinism became clearer. The Jews of the 20th century were no different from others of European-American background, having in a time of crisis to choose between nationalism, socialism, and liberalism or a combination of two or all three of these.
